Web3 Concept Corner

01

What is Gwei?

Gwei is a micro-denomination of Ether (ETH), standing for "Giga-Wei" (1 Gwei = 1,000,000,000 Wei, or 10-9 ETH). Just as pennies represent fractions of dollars, Gwei measures gas prices, making it easy to express small transaction costs.

02

How EIP-1559 Works

Ethereum uses EIP-1559 since 2021. Transaction fees are split into two: a Base Fee (set by the protocol depending on network congestion, and permanently burned to decrease ETH supply) and a Priority Fee / Tip (optional extra fee given directly to validators to speed up transaction inclusion).

03

Why Gas Fluctuates

Gas prices shift based on demand for blockspace. During high-intensity moments—like popular NFT mints, major market swings, or DeFi yield rushes—hundreds of users submit transactions concurrently. Since blocks have a maximum capacity, users tip higher amounts to outbid others, driving up base fees.
